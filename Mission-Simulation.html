Mission Simulation for Subsystem-Level R&D

ASET Core enables engineers to simulate airplane and weapon subsystem behavior across realistic mission profiles. The focus is on propulsion, fuel, controls, thermal, mechanical, and electrical systems. No theater-level operations or orbital mechanics.

Mission Environment

Define ground, air, or ocean terrain modes with configurable weather, atmosphere, and altitude bands. Simulate subsystem performance under realistic conditions without global map overhead.

  • Standard or custom atmosphere profiles
  • Wind, temperature, and turbulence layers
  • Runway and carrier deck options

Mission Timeline & Events

Missions follow time-based sequences like takeoff, climb, cruise, and descent. Engineers can introduce events that stress subsystems or change vehicle configuration.

  • Throttle schedules and power demands
  • Subsystem transitions and failure modes
  • Coupled simulation across all systems

Subsystem Coupling

ASET Core maintains a shared state across all subsystems, enabling real-time interaction between propulsion, fuel, controls, and thermal models. This reveals integration risks and tradeoffs that siloed tools miss.

Mission Outputs

Engineers receive time histories and summary metrics to support design decisions and trade studies. Outputs emphasize subsystem performance, margins, and health.

  • Range, endurance, and fuel reserves
  • Thrust and power profiles
  • Thermal and electrical loading vs. limits
  • Subsystem health indicators

Deliberate Scope

ASET Core is not a replacement for STK or campaign simulators. It excludes orbital mechanics, global maps, and tactical coordination. Instead, it fills the missing layer between subsystem modeling and mission performance. ASET Core is focused, modular, and built for engineering R&D.

Navigator — Optional Built-In Assistant

Navigator is ASET Core’s optional assistant that helps users move through mission setup, subsystem configuration, and simulation workflows with clarity. It explains errors, surfaces documentation, and highlights mission-level insights without replacing engineering judgment.
